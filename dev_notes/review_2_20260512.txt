I've read the codebase (~8k LOC core + ~600 LOC schema + ~10 dev_notes files) and the memory. Here's the new-staff-dev take:

Top-line critique
The tool has built sophisticated machinery for problems it doesn't yet have, while serving an editorial audience of one (Lisa-shape Guardian journalism). Most of the "framework-y" infrastructure was right at one moment, then got patched as the moment shifted, instead of being torn out. Phase 6.0.5 / 6.0.6 / 6.0.7 / 6.3 / 6.4 / 6.5 / 6.6 / 6.9 / 6.10 is the giveaway — sub-decimal phases mean the foundations are still moving, not stabilising.

Concrete assumption stacks
Method-version strings + append-plus-supersede chain. hs_group_yoy_v8_excludes_gb_reporter_pre_brexit, hs_group_trajectory_v7_longest_contiguous_run, natural_key_hash, value_signature, superseded_at, superseded_by_finding_id, last_confirmed_at, findings_io.py as the canonical write path. The project's own project memory admits the live chain was wiped at the Phase 5 rebuild because it was "mostly dev-churn artefacts, not editorial revision history." A new dev would ask: who reads the supersede chain? If the answer is "no journalist, ever" — and it is — the chain is paying interest on a debt no editorial reader will collect.
The caveat catalogue is in tension with itself. 14 caveat codes in lookups.py, six of which fire on every active hs_group_yoy finding. briefing_pack.py:309 (_section_universal_caveats) lifts them to the brief header, and SUPPRESSED_INLINE_CAVEATS suppresses them inline. So the system attaches the caveats per-finding, then suppresses them per-finding, then re-renders them globally. The honest version: if it's universal, it's a footer; only carry per-row what actually varies per row.
Multi-partner scope is two questions in one column. (CN, HK, MO) vs CN is a real editorial choice (transshipment-aware vs Merics/Soapbox-comparable). Carrying it as a scope field on every finding × every flow × every analyser means the summary tab in sheets_export.py is "wide, one row per HS group, all 3 scopes × 2 flows side by side." That's 6 columns per metric. Pick one default (CN+HK+MO is right — transshipment is the editorial point), drop the plumbing, and run a one-off CN-only export the rare time a story actually needs the narrow figure.
Three editorial surfaces over one finding set. findings.md + leads.md + data.xlsx, all citing the same finding/N tokens. The leads.md split happened four days ago over a "telephone-game" worry about downstream LLMs reading nested LLM prose. The fix to "don't feed LLM prose to another LLM" is "don't feed it" — not "duplicate the bundle." briefing_pack.py is 2,001 lines partly because it renders the same data into both Markdown shapes.
"Journalist-configurable hs_groups" abstraction with one journalist. 29 hs_groups, all seeded by Luke; the draft: / seed: prefix is metadata only Luke reads; the promote-or-drop pass is a self-curation ritual. There's no second journalist editing the watchlist. Hardcoding the live set in schema.sql loses a configurability hardly anyone uses and removes a whole mental layer.
LLM layer at high architectural altitude for low yield. llm_framing.py is 991 lines: hypothesis catalog of 12, fact pre-formatting, post-hoc number extraction, sign-aware match with magnitude-only fallback, currency-not-sign-flipped-by-decrease-verbs, hallucination rejection — to produce 28 paragraphs. The discipline is exemplary; the question is whether the discipline justifies the layer's existence at all, or whether a journalist with the brief reads the prose at "huh, neat" level then ignores it.
Forward-work backlog has its own gravity. UK data gap, 2018 GACC parser (PNGs!), Q&A bot, web UI, GoogleSheetsWriter, eu_bloc aggregate analyser, CN8 concordance table, structural-break detection, per-reporter rollup. Each defensible in isolation; together they describe a tool growing past its single user.
What "more true, less functional" would look like
Cut the supersede chain. Re-running an analyser truncates and replaces. Audit trail = git log over an exports/ folder of dated brief bundles. Loses: theoretical ability to query "what changed about this finding over revisions." Gains: ~200 lines of findings_io.py, the method_v8_... naming convention, the natural-key hash machinery, and the conceptual overhead.
Collapse leads.md back into a section of findings.md, or delete the LLM layer. Pick one. Either it's editorially load-bearing (then it goes in the brief and you tell journalists not to pipe leads to NotebookLM), or it's optional decoration (then 991 lines is a lot for decoration).
One scope, not three. CN+HK+MO is the journalistically correct default for "Chinese trade to Europe." Drop scope as a finding column. The wide summary tab becomes half as wide. Loses: easy side-by-side; gains: every analyser collapses by a meaningful fraction.
Hardcode the hs_groups. Until journalist #2 turns up, "configurable" is theatre. Move them from a DB table to a Python literal; lose the draft/promote ritual.
Drop the universal caveats from per-finding rows. Methodology footer once per brief. caveat_codes array on findings keeps only the ones that vary.
Freeze the forward-work backlog. Reopen items only when a specific Lisa story is blocked on them. Most won't be.
After cuts the core would be: ingest (scrape + eurostat + fx) → observations → ~3 deterministic analysers → one Markdown brief + one spreadsheet. Probably ~3,500 lines instead of ~8,000.

What I'd keep, untouched
Schema layering (eurostat_raw_rows → observations with FK array back → findings). This is the genuinely "true" part — every claim traceable to verbatim source rows is the editorial guarantee, and it's cheap.
Pre-registered shock + Soapbox validation discipline. The 2018 steel "predicted +, got −21%" outcome is exactly the kind of methodology rigour journalism needs. Don't touch.
emit_finding as a single write path. Even if the supersede machinery goes, having one canonical write point is the right shape.
Theil-Sen over OLS, longest-contiguous-run for trajectories, low-base flagging. These are the analytical choices that earn their keep — they exist because OLS / first-and-last / unguarded YoY produced editorially wrong answers, and the fixes are well-motivated and small.
The single sharpest cut
If I had to pick one: delete the supersede chain plus method-version naming. It's the most visible signal of "we built infrastructure for journalists that journalists don't use" and removing it pulls a thread that simplifies findings_io.py, anomalies.py's 35 functions, the schema, and the mental model of every new analyser at once.
